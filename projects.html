Prior to attending The University of Colorado Boulder, I had no experience involving anything related to Computer Science. With no knowledge of coding or the mindset required for it, I needed to be perserverant through all of my coursework in order to succeed. Through my school work and attending 'Hackathons' hosted in my community, I have been able to expose myself to lots of new elements of the field. This perserverance is what lead me to discover what I love about coding, and more specifically, design.
Below are some of the projects I am most proud of and grateful to have accomplished through my learning career.

OptiFilms

I worked on a team to create a media streaming search platform, the purpose of which was for the user to be able to put in the name of a TV show or movie, and the website would return what streaming platforms you could find the media on. It took the form of a website but we also wanted to make it into an app! This was the first website that I had the opportunity to create from the ground up, and is one of my greatest achievements!

Local GitHub

For one of my school projects, we were tasked with replicating some of GitHub's commands locally, like that of git pull, add, commit, stash, and others. The goal was to test our understanding of different data structures present in object oriented design. Using linked lists, a partner and I were able to create our own version of GitHub, which also further advanced our understanding of the platform itself.

Happy Messages!

Happy Messages intended for the users to input their phone number into the website, and everyday at an allotted time they would receive an encouraging text message to help them through the day! Inspired by the prompt “How would you make the world a better place?” I thought that sometimes people just need a happy message to get through the day.
